Next Gen Sounds

Teens learn by running a real music company.

This isn’t a classroom model. Students operate inside a real record label environment powered by The Artist’s Cut — creating music, releasing projects, managing campaigns, and learning business by actually doing it.

MODULE 01

Creative & Technical

Students build the product. This phase focuses on creation — music, media, and content — using modern tools and AI-assisted workflows.

  • AI music generation and prompt engineering
  • Audio production, mixing, and release prep
  • Video production, editing, and storytelling
  • Content packaging for real distribution

What they actually do

Students don’t just learn tools — they create real songs, real visuals, and real release-ready assets that move directly into the label pipeline.

This is the difference

Students are not simulating a business — they are operating inside one. Releases, campaigns, and financial decisions are real.

MODULE 02

Business & Operations

Students run the label. They manage distribution, analyze data, and execute campaigns tied to real artist releases.

  • Distribution workflows (metadata, uploads, DSP delivery)
  • Marketing campaigns and audience growth
  • Royalties, splits, and financial literacy
  • Operational roles inside the label
MODULE 03

Professional & Leadership

This phase builds the operator. Students develop discipline, leadership, and the mindset required to succeed in real environments.

  • Leadership, accountability, and communication
  • Team-based project execution
  • Mentorship from industry professionals
  • Pathway to "Graduate Operator" roles

End result

Students leave with real experience, real output, and the ability to operate inside a creative business — not just knowledge.

How It Works

A connected pathway from learning to leadership

Step 01

Learn the tools

Students are introduced to AI music workflows, production, media creation, release systems, and professional expectations.

Step 02

Operate the work

Participants move into real assignments tied to active music, marketing, and platform operations.

Step 03

Grow into leadership

Advanced participants take on more ownership, mentorship responsibilities, and stronger operator roles.
